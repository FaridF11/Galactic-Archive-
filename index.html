The foundation

What Star Wars Is About

Mythic family drama

At its center, Star Wars follows families, mentors, apprentices, and chosen identities under the pressure of galactic war. The Skywalkers are the main spine, but the saga constantly widens to show ordinary pilots, rebels, senators, clones, smugglers, mystics, and citizens shaping history.

The Force

The Force is an energy field generated by living things. Jedi seek harmony, restraint, compassion, and defense. Sith pursue domination through fear, anger, and attachment twisted into possession. Most conflicts in the saga mirror that spiritual divide.

Politics and empire

The Republic falls through corruption, emergency power, and a manufactured war. The Empire rises by promising order while using terror, surveillance, military occupation, and superweapons. Rebellions and resistance movements become the moral counterweight.

Used future design

Star Wars feels tactile: worn ships, scratched droids, dusty settlements, brutalist bases, ceremonial temples, and cities layered over older civilizations. Technology is advanced, but it is rarely pristine. History is visible in the metal.

Chronological map

The Canon Eras

1

Dawn of the Jedi

The earliest known age of Force study, before the Jedi Order becomes the institution seen in the films. It establishes ancient temples, first teachings, and the idea that Force traditions are older than galactic governments.

2

The Old Republic

A legendary period of Jedi and Sith wars, ancient republics, lost civilizations, and traditions remembered through ruins, holocrons, and myths. Canon often references it as deep history rather than fully dramatizing it.

3

The High Republic

The Jedi operate near their cultural height. The Republic expands into the Outer Rim, the Jedi are public guardians, and threats such as the Nihil expose the limits of idealism, infrastructure, and trust.

4

Fall of the Jedi

The prequel era. Senator Palpatine engineers crisis, war, and fear. The Clone Wars exhaust the Republic, the Jedi become military commanders, Anakin Skywalker falls, and the Empire is born.

5

Reign of the Empire

The Imperial age. Jedi survivors hide, systems are occupied, clones are phased out, and resistance cells form. The Death Star project becomes the symbol of rule through overwhelming terror.

6

Age of Rebellion

The original trilogy era. Luke, Leia, Han, Chewbacca, Lando, droids, pilots, spies, and soldiers help transform scattered resistance into a military and moral challenge to the Empire.

7

New Republic and First Order

After Endor, the New Republic struggles to demilitarize and govern. Imperial remnants reorganize into the First Order. Rey, Finn, Poe, Rose, and Leia's Resistance confront a new authoritarian threat and an old Sith design.

Core systems

Lore, Rules, and Conflicts

The Force in detail

The Force connects living beings, places, memory, instinct, and destiny. Jedi training emphasizes calm perception, service, and letting go of possessive fear. Dark side practice gives quick power because it feeds on intense emotion, but it narrows the user's world until control replaces connection. Force ghosts show that selfless surrender can preserve consciousness without clinging to life.

Common abilities include precognition, telekinesis, heightened reflexes, mind influence, sensing emotions, healing, and communion with the dead. Rare or dangerous practices include Sith alchemy, essence transfer, Force lightning, dyads, and artifact-bound knowledge.

Jedi Order

Peacekeepers trained from youth, organized by temples, councils, masters, knights, and padawans. Their strengths are discipline and service. Their weakness is institutional blindness when politics and war reshape their role.

Sith Order

Dark side tradition built around power, secrecy, domination, and succession by betrayal. Darth Bane's Rule of Two reduces Sith infighting into one master and one apprentice until Palpatine reveals the final trap.

Clone Wars

A manufactured conflict between the Republic and Separatists. Both armies are shaped by Sith planning: clones on one side, battle droids on the other, and emergency powers feeding Palpatine's rise.

Hyperspace

Ships travel faster than light through calculated routes. Navigation matters because gravity wells, mass shadows, interdiction fields, and bad coordinates can destroy a vessel or strand it in hostile space.

Droids

Droids range from protocol interpreters and astromechs to assassins and tactical units. They are tools in law, but many display memory, loyalty, humor, fear, and personality shaped by experience.

Superweapons

The Death Stars, Starkiller Base, and planet-killing fleets show authoritarian thinking at its most extreme: reduce politics to fear, then mistake terror for permanent control.

How to watch

Viewing Order and Complete Screen Checklist

This guide separates the main story path from everything else available to watch. The core order below follows the in-universe timeline; anthology shows are marked because some episodes jump around. Current as June 5, 2026, checked against the official StarWars.com movies and series viewing guide.

Best First-Time Path

Start with release order if you have never seen Star Wars: Episodes IV-VI, I-III, then VII-IX, adding Rogue One and Solo after the prequels. This preserves the original reveals and lets the filmmaking evolve naturally.

Use chronological order when you want the story as galactic history. That is the order below, with shows placed where they fit.

Clone Wars Tip

The Clone Wars was released out of timeline order. For the cleanest story, watch the 2008 movie, then the series using the official chronological episode order before moving into Revenge of the Sith and The Bad Batch.

The Tales anthology shows can be watched after you know the characters they focus on, even though their episodes occur across multiple eras.

Places and powers

Worlds, Factions, and Technology

Tatooine

Desert world in the Outer Rim, ruled more by survival, moisture farming, criminal power, and local tribes than central government. It shapes Anakin and Luke through isolation, hardship, and longing.

Coruscant

Galactic capital, city-planet, Senate seat, Jedi Temple home, and visual symbol of civilization layered over inequality. Its skyline hides political decay underneath ceremonial grandeur.

Naboo

Elegant world of humans and Gungans. Its invasion launches the prequel crisis, introduces Padme and Palpatine's political opportunity, and contrasts beauty with manipulation.

Mandalore

A warrior culture split by pacifism, clan identity, occupation, exile, and myth. The Darksaber becomes a contested symbol of leadership rather than a simple crown.

Rebel Alliance

A coalition of senators, spies, soldiers, defectors, smugglers, and citizens. Its strength is distributed courage; its challenge is balancing secrecy, sacrifice, and hope.

Galactic Empire

A centralized dictatorship built from the Republic's legal machinery. It uses governors, stormtroopers, intelligence, propaganda, fear, and industrial scale to hold systems in line.

First Order

Imperial remnants transformed into a militarized successor state. It relies on indoctrination, hidden buildup, fanatic command, and resentment toward the New Republic.

Lightsabers

Jedi and Sith ritual weapons powered by kyber crystals. Blade color often signals tradition, construction, corruption, or personal path: blue and green for Jedi practice, red for bled crystals, white for purified ones.
